Ranger

2001 Ford Ranger Edge

Vehicle Overview

3rd-gen Ford Ranger (1998–2012). The 3.0L Vulcan V6 is an OHV pushrod engine — reliable, simple, dirt-cheap parts. RWD with 5R55E automatic.

NJ Owner Notes

Body-on-frame trucks in NJ need regular frame inspections. Check spring perches, brake/fuel lines, crossmembers for salt rot. Undercoat annually.

Civic

2019 Honda Civic Sport

Vehicle Overview

10th-gen Civic with the NA 2.0L R20A i-VTEC and Honda's own CVT. No turbo, no oil dilution — straightforward DOHC four-cylinder. CVT has a better track record than Jatco units.

DIY Friendly

Most maintenance is basic DIY. CVT fluid: Honda HCF-2 every 30–40k miles.

Rogue

2016 Nissan Rogue

Vehicle Overview

2nd-gen Rogue (T32) with 2.5L QR25DE and Jatco CVT. Engine is reliable. The Jatco CVT is the known weak point — documented premature failure history in 2014–2017 models.

CVT Warning

Change CVT fluid every 30k with Nissan NS-3 only. No generic fluid. Consider an external cooler for heavy use.

Guide

Pre-Purchase Inspection

Before you even start the engine — get under it.

1

Frame Rails

Tap with a screwdriver. Solid thunk = good. Hollow crunch = rot. #1 deal-breaker in NJ.

2

Rear Spring Perches

Where leaf springs bolt to frame. NJ trucks rot here first. If gone, walk away.

3

Brake & Fuel Lines

Steel lines along the frame. Salt turns them to lace. Replaceable DIY — factor into negotiation.

4

Floor Pans

Poke from underneath. Rust-free metal resists. Screwdriver goes through = problem.

5

Transmission Fluid

Red/pink = good. Dark brown or burnt smell = walk away or negotiate hard.

Negotiation

Show up with cash at your target. Moderate frame rust → knock $500–700 off. Dark trans fluid → $1,000+ off. Rotted frame → walk away at any price.

Ranger

2001 Ford Ranger Edge

Vehicle Overview

3rd-gen Ranger (1998–2012). 3.0L Vulcan V6 — OHV pushrod, reliable, simple, cheap parts. RWD with 5R55E automatic.

NJ Owner Notes

Body-on-frame + NJ salt = inspect frame annually. Spring perches, brake/fuel lines, crossmembers rot first. Undercoat every fall.

Civic

2019 Honda Civic Sport

Vehicle Overview

10th-gen with NA 2.0L R20A i-VTEC and Honda's own CVT. No turbo, no oil dilution. Better CVT reliability than Jatco units.

DIY Friendly

Most maintenance is basic. CVT fluid: Honda HCF-2 every 30–40k miles.

Rogue

2016 Nissan Rogue

Vehicle Overview

2nd-gen (T32) with 2.5L QR25DE and Jatco CVT. Engine is solid. The Jatco CVT is the weak point — documented failures in 2014–2017.

⚠ CVT Warning

Change fluid every 30k with Nissan NS-3 only. No generic. External cooler recommended for heavy summer driving.

Guide

Pre-Purchase Inspection

Before you start the engine — get under it

1

Frame Rails

Tap with screwdriver. Solid thunk = good. Hollow crunch = rot. #1 NJ deal-breaker.

2

Rear Spring Perches

Where leaf springs bolt to frame. Rots first in NJ. Gone = walk away.

3

Brake & Fuel Lines

Steel lines along frame. Salt turns them to lace. DIY replaceable — factor into price.

4

Floor Pans

Poke from underneath. Rust-free metal resists. Screwdriver goes through = problem.

5

Transmission Fluid

Red/pink = good. Dark brown or burnt = walk away or negotiate hard.

Negotiation

Cash at target price. Moderate rust → $500–700 off. Dark trans fluid → $1,000+ off. Rotted frame → walk at any price.
